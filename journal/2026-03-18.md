# 25 things to do to radically lower your expenses  

It starts with the deepest root causes you highlighted—removing the “addictions” (both obvious and subtle) that quietly drive spending through impulses, health issues, convenience-seeking, and emotional voids—then moves to practical cuts. These early steps create the biggest long-term leverage because they stop the leaks at the source.

Work on what you can work on, but prioritize your focus on the big things first ... so that you have more resources to attack the lower priorities ... but overall ... knock off the easy, low-hanging fruit right away.

Start with the first 5–10 items—the mindset, substance, and health shifts. They remove the hidden drivers that make the later cuts feel effortless and sustainable. Once those foundations are in place, the practical steps become simple maintenance rather than constant willpower battles. Many people see their biggest expense drops within the first 30–60 days of tackling the early items alone.

1. Cultivate centeredness and a life of faith through daily spiritual practices (prayer, meditation, or reflection).  
   Inner peace replaces the need to buy happiness, status, or distraction—eliminating most counseling, shopping therapy and any kind of impulse spending.

2. Quit ALL illicit drugs and seek support if needed -- it's not just the immediate gains, as one goes on in life, one cannot afford to have a drug conviction on one's record ... so it's NOT JUST about you, it's about the example you set for younger kids who are connected to you.  
   Removes a massive direct cost plus legal, health, and productivity drains.

3. Eliminate alcohol consumption. TAPER it off. AVOID friends who drink or induce your drinking. Be a better example for them, even if you have to risk being preachy.  
   Cut bar tabs out entirely, then home stock, and ALL of the myriad poor decisions that lead to extra spending while intoxicated or while hanging out with idiots who need to drink to be entertained.

4. Stop using tobacco, nicotine, or vaping products.  
   One of the highest-ROI quits—hundreds or thousands saved yearly on products and related health issues.

5. Transform your diet to eliminate high blood sugar triggers—cut added sugar and ultra-processed foods, then meal-prep wholesome options.  
   Lowers grocery bills dramatically and prevents future medical bills from diet-related problems.

6. COMMIT to a regular fitness routine using free methods (walking, bodyweight workouts, outdoor exercise). It is notoriously easy to get gym memberships and then never use them -- instead do things like gardening/landscaping that provide the motivation to regular do physical chores. 
   Builds energy, avoids gym memberships or future doctor visits caused by inactivity.

7. Establish excellent sleep hygiene (consistent schedule, no screens before bed, dark/cool room).  
   Better rest means less reliance on caffeine, energy drinks, pills, or “quick-fix” conveniences that cost money.

8. Increase mobility through daily walking, biking, stretching, or active commuting. If possible, take the insurance off your vehicle so that your vehicle never leaves the garage. 
   ELIMINATING an automotive lifestyle slashes gas, parking, insurance, rideshare, and car-maintenance bills.

9. Break gadget addiction by setting strict no-buy rules and decluttering what you already own.  
   Ends the upgrade cycle and the constant desire for the “latest” version of everything.

10. Let go of excessive comfort-seeking by embracing simpler living (lower thermostat, fewer conveniences, close to ambient temperature regulation).  
    Directly cuts utility bills and stops buying items just to make life artificially easy.

11. Adopt a 30-day wait rule for any non-essential purchase. In general, avoid high convenience and ultra-processed foods. 
    Kills shopping addiction and impulse buys that quietly add up.

12. Cancel or downgrade every non-essential subscription and membership (streaming, apps, boxes, unused gyms).  
    Instant recurring savings—often $50–200+ per month with zero lifestyle hit.

13. Stop eating out, ordering delivery, or buying prepared, convenient or ultra-processed foods; cook and meal-prep at home ... try FASTING!  
    One of the single biggest household expense categories for most people.

14. Track every penny spent for 30+ days, but ideally, you need to switch to a zero-based budget. Just STOP spending any money EVER ... only for exceptions. 
    Awareness alone stops leaks you didn’t even realize existed.

15. Reduce transportation costs by switching to walking, Ubering, public transit, biking, carpooling, or getting rides.  
    Targets another top-three expense category for dramatic cuts.

16. Negotiate or shop around for lower rates on insurance, phone, internet, and utilities. Or just remove them and see if you can live without. 
    Quick wins that can save hundreds per year with a few calls.

17. Downsize your living space whenever the opportunity presents itself; REFUSE to spend more on a better/larger house or fixing up your house.  
    Housing, including home repair, is usually the largest single expense—right-sizing it creates huge breathing room.

18. Cut energy and utility bills through simple efficiency habits; AVOID buying anything -- turn lights off, use the HVAC less, try to use zero power.  
    Ongoing savings that require almost no upfront cost.

19. Shop only with lists, buy generic/store brands, secondhand, thrift, and only on sale.  
    Reduces grocery and household-goods spending by 30–50% for many.

20. Practice asceticim or at least minimalism—declutter regularly, sell unused items, NEVER buy non-essentials.  
    Generates cash from sales and prevents future accumulation.

21. Replace paid entertainment with free or low-cost alternatives (libraries, parks, hiking, DIY hobbies, free community events).  
    Keeps life rich without the entertainment budget drain.

22. Switch to a capsule wardrobe and buy clothes rarely (only secondhand or on deep sale).  
    STOP associating with anyone who pushes you into fashion-driven spending.

23. Prepare coffee, tea, lunches, and snacks at home instead of buying them out.  
    Small daily habits that compound into hundreds saved per year.

24. Unsubscribe, UNFOLLOW, UNFRIEND.
    Reduce the external pressure that fuels any kind of unnecessary purchase or change.

25. Prioritize paying off consumer debt to eliminate interest payments, and review banking/insurance for fee-free options. MOSTLY, it's important to STOP EVER NEEDING MONEY. IF YOU CAN'T LIVE WITHOUT MONEY, you have not really worked hard enough on the NUMBER ONE priority on this list yet. 
    Stops the silent wealth destroyer (interest) and optimizes every remaining bill.
