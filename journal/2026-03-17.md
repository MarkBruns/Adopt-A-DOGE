# What's the Story With LOWER Cancer Rates of Farmers In Iowa

1) Cancer is a metabolic disease in which tumor cells preferentially ferment glucose for energy (the Warburg effect), even when oxygen is available. Sugar itself does not initiate cancer, but elevated blood glucose directly fuels tumor growth, proliferation, and metastasis. Active lifestyles and physically demanding work rapidly clear circulating glucose through muscle and organ uptake, limiting the fuel available to cancer cells and dramatically lowering metastatic risk—high-intensity aerobic exercise alone has been shown to reduce it by up to 72%.

2) In Iowa, a large share of carcinogen exposure likely stems from older, affluent homeowners who over-apply lawn weed killers and herbicides without reading labels or following safety guidelines, transferring high residues to grandchildren via skin, clothing, and home dust. By contrast, agricultural pesticides are handled almost exclusively by certified, trained applicators under strict regulatory oversight; misapplications are rare, quickly addressed through neighbor complaints, liability threats, and community enforcement. No equivalent training or accountability exists for residential users, who often escape personal consequences while exposing younger family members.

3) In counties where people routinely live into their 90s, cancer incidence among the very elderly naturally appears higher because those who have already survived cardiovascular disease and other early killers simply reach the ages at which cancer becomes the dominant threat—due to competing risks of mortality. When other causes of death are minimized, cancer emerges as the more likely endpoint.

The references can be verified via PubMed, EPA, or the Iowa Cancer Registry for anyone wishing to challenge the data. These are the core peer-reviewed, governmental, and registry sources that directly underpin the three arguments. All links are publicly accessible (PubMed/PMC, journal sites, or official Iowa government pages) and can be verified or challenged independently.


### Cancer Metabolism & Exercise (Supporting Argument 1)

1. **Liberti MV, Locasale JW.** The Warburg Effect: How Does it Benefit Cancer Cells? *Trends in Biochemical Sciences*. 2016;41(3):211-218. https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4783224/  

2. **Vander Heiden MG, Cantley LC, Thompson CB.** Understanding the Warburg effect: the metabolic requirements of cell proliferation. *Science*. 2009;324(5930):1029-1033. https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1160809  

3. **Sheinboim D, et al.** (Carmit Levy group). An Exercise-Induced Metabolic Shield in Distant Organs Blocks Cancer Progression and Metastatic Dissemination. *Cancer Research*. 2022;82(22):4164-4178. https://aacrjournals.org/cancerres/article/82/22/4164/710131 (Human epidemiological data show high-intensity aerobic exercise linked to ~72% lower metastatic cancer risk via glucose uptake competition.)

### Pesticide Exposure (Residential vs. Agricultural – Supporting Argument 2)

4. **Roberts JR, Karr CJ; Council on Environmental Health.** Pesticide Exposure in Children. *Pediatrics*. 2012;130(6):e1765-e1788. https://publications.aap.org/pediatrics/article/130/6/e1765/32543/Pesticide-Exposure-in-Children (Details track-in of lawn herbicides to homes/clothing and elevated exposure risks to children/grandchildren.)  

5. **Iowa Department of Agriculture and Land Stewardship (IDALS).** Pesticide Use Investigations and Enforcement. Official guidelines on commercial applicator certification, mandatory training, investigations, fines, and neighbor-driven enforcement. https://iowaagriculture.gov/pesticide-bureau/pesticide-use-investigations-and-enforcement
