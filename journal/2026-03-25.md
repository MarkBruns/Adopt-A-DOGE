## Chronological Timeline of Iran Illustrates Cultural/Development Lessons

**Core Dynamics In The History of Oil: Lac Of Meritocracy, Geopolitics, Revenue Flow, Development Failure**: Oil’s centrality to technological societies and defense drove British/US interventions to secure it from USSR. Early foreign concessions necessary due to Iran’s pre-industrial institutional/cultural deficits (no secure property rights, capital markets, or entrepreneurial tradition). Later revenues concentrated among elites, funding weapons/repression over inclusive development — perpetuating failure to build domestic capabilities or broad prosperity, fueling revolution.

## Iran Ancient–19th Century
Natural petroleum seeps/hand-dug wells used locally for waterproofing, medicine, lighting, mortar. Qajar economy backward: agrarian/tribal, arbitrary rule threatening capital accumulation, Islamic inheritance laws fragmenting assets, no corporations/capital markets, traditional/religious education (not empirical/technical), declining crafts vs. imports. Institutional/cultural path dependence prevented indigenous entrepreneurs, drillers, explorers, engineers, or project organization skills — no environment empowering risk-taking industrial ambition like Western titans (Rockefeller/British pioneers). Early concessions (e.g., 1872 Reuter) cancelled; no local tech base.

## 1901
**D’Arcy Concession**: Qajar Shah grants British William Knox D’Arcy 60-year exclusive rights (excl. north). Oil now essential backbone of *technological societies* (industrial fuel, transport, chemicals) and *defense capabilities* (modern navies, mechanized warfare). **Foreign tech/capital mandatory**: Iran lacked drilling expertise, geologists, infrastructure, massive venture capital, and organizational skills due to above institutional deficits (weak property rights, no corporate law, feudal/tribal fragmentation) and cultural/historical lag (no tradition of scale industrial entrepreneurship). Shahs sold rights for immediate cash amid fiscal crisis; no domestic "get after it" capability emerged.

## 1908–1909
Oil struck Masjed Soleyman; **Anglo-Persian Oil Company (APOC)** formed. Revenues skewed to British owners amid harsh terrain, disease, hostile tribes/Shia resistance to outsiders.

## 1914
British govt acquires 51% APOC for Royal Navy fuel security — oil as strategic defense asset enabling empire power projection.

## 1921–1925
Reza Shah rises. Oil royalties fund limited modernization but concentrate among ruling elite (rentier pattern begins).

## 1933
Revised concession still favors Anglo-Iranian Oil Company (AIOC).

## 1941
**Anglo-Soviet invasion** secures oil fields/refinery for Allied technological warfare logistics.

## 1945–1946
**Iran Crisis**: Soviets seek northern oil; West forces withdrawal to deny USSR control of resource powering industrial/military expansion.

## 1951
**Mohammad Mossadegh** nationalizes AIOC. Resentment over foreign/elite skew; Western fears of revenue diversion undermining reliable supply for global tech/defense needs.

## 1953
**CIA/MI6 coup** restores Shah to protect Western access to strategic oil asset.

## 1953–1978
Shah’s White Revolution/oil boom. Revenues exploded but **rentier state effect** (state captured "free" oil rents without broad taxation or accountability). There was massive failure of leadership because the monarch was reactionary and could not understand the long-term incentive to build financial and educational institutions, to develop human capital and the next generation of leaders, or do anything to encourage entrepreneurial culture/leadership skills necessary for future generations of wealth creation, ie after the oil is gone. Massive diversion to expensive, sophisticated arms purchases (5th-largest military), SAVAK repression ("thug forces"), elite patronage/corruption rather than diversified investment or "rising tide that lifts all boats" prosperity. Top-down imports of tech failed to create organic Iranian ambitions/organization (no Rockefeller equivalents); inequality, inflation, dependency grew — illustrating how oil rents reinforced institutional/cultural lags instead of fostering broad economic assets.

## 1979
Shah flees; Khomeini returns to lead islamic extremists; monarchy collapses; no credible competing force to islamic extremists emerges from Iran people. **IRGC** formed by ayatollah decree in power vacuum. Theocratic Republic via referendum/constitution. No democratic elections for core power; IRGC/clergy brutally suppress rivals ... thus outdoing the Shah in FAILURE to do anything to encourage entrepreneurial culture/leadership skills necessary for future generations of wealth creation, ie after the oil is gone. Perhaps even worse or more stupid than failing the Iranian people, the IRGC invests in fomenting terrorism with a highly-distributed global network, prototypically including entities like [Hezbollah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hezbollah).
